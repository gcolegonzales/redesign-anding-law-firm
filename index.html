Meet Your Attorney

George K. Anding, Jr.

Trial Attorney · Anding Law Firm, L.L.C.

“Buzzy” Anding has spent five decades on the plaintiff’s side of the toughest injury cases in Louisiana. He clerked for a United States District Judge, went into practice in 1974, and has been trying medical-negligence cases ever since.

He is board certified in Medical Professional Liability by the American Board of Professional Liability Attorneys — a distinction few Louisiana lawyers hold — and carries Martindale-Hubbell’s highest AV Preeminent rating for legal ability and ethics. He was named to Super Lawyers in 2007 and again from 2009 through 2012.

When you hire this firm, George is the lawyer on your case — not a name on the door. He reads the medical records himself, explains where you actually stand, and prepares the file for trial from day one.

What To Expect

From your first call to a resolution.

Injury cases are stressful and unfamiliar. Here is how the firm works so you always know what comes next.

  1. 1

    Free, confidential case review

    Call or send the form. George reviews what happened at no cost and tells you honestly whether there’s a case worth pursuing.

  2. 2

    Records, experts & investigation

    The firm gathers the medical records, consults the right specialists, and builds the factual and expert record a malpractice or injury claim requires.

  3. 3

    Demand & negotiation

    With the evidence assembled, we press for a full and fair resolution — from a position of strength, because the file is already trial-ready.

  4. 4

    Trial, if that’s what it takes

    If the other side won’t be reasonable, George is prepared to try your case to a judge or jury — the experience that makes the difference at every earlier stage.

Injury and malpractice cases are typically handled on a contingency fee — you generally owe no attorney’s fee unless there is a recovery. Discuss the specifics of any fee arrangement during your consultation.
